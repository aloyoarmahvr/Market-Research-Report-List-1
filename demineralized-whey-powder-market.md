<p><p>Demineralized whey powder is categorized based on its demineralization levels, defined by the percentage of mineral content removed. D-40 has 40% mineral removal, D-50 has 50%, D-70 targets 70%, and D-90 achieves 90% demineralization. Each type serves different applications in the food and nutrition sectors; for instance, higher D-values like D-90 are often preferred for infant formula and dietary supplements due to their lower mineral content, while lower D-values may be used in bakery and dairy products. Other markets may include specialized applications tailored to specific industry needs.</p></p>
